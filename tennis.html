Tennis Sisters

Serena Williams

Serena Williams is a revolutionary female tennis player. She has won more Grand Slam singles titles than any other man or woman during the open era. S She grew up in Compton, California and she and her sister, Venus, was coached by her father at an early age. She began her debut at 16 in 1998 in the Australian opener. She won her first match, but her sister beat her in the second round. This was the match that brought their 'rivalry' to the spotlight. When Serena was 17, she won her first Grand Slam, making her one of the youngest female tennis players to win a Major. She boasts this set of achievements:

  • 23 Grand Slam titles
  • 4 Olympic Gold Medals
  • 14 Grand Slam Doubles titles
  • Won the French Open thrice

Only Margaret Court has won more Grand Slams than her (24). She is planning to retire soon, and many believe this will be an end to an era.

Venus Williams

Venus Williams is the sister to Serena Willams, and was coached by her father at a young age. She became a professional in 1994 and she gained attention for her strenght exhibited in her serves and groundstrokes. She became the first unseeded US Open women's finalist in the open era in 1997. In 2000, she won both Wimbledon and the US Open. Even after she suffered many injuries, she continued to win her fourth Wimbledon in 2007.
